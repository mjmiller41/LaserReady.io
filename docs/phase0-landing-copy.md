## The problem (section)

**You bought the file. It still didn't cut right.**

You find out the same way every time — after the material's already on the bed. The outline had a gap, so the
part never released. Two lines sat on top of each other and double-burned. A "cut" layer came in as engrave.
A curve was actually 4,000 nodes and the head stuttered the whole way around. None of it showed on screen.

Marketplace files, AI-generated files, hand-traced files — they all hit the same wall, because "looks right in
a preview" and "cuts right on a laser" are two different things.

## What LaserReady does (section)

**LaserReady reads your file the way your laser will, and flags the problems first.**

Upload an SVG or DXF from anywhere — Etsy, a design bundle, an AI generator, your own software. LaserReady

It doesn't care where the file came from. That's the point — every other tool only checks files *it* made.

## How it works (3 steps)

1. **Upload.** SVG or DXF, from any source. Tell us your machine and material thickness (optional, but it
   sharpens the check).
2. **We inspect.** LaserReady checks path integrity, scale, layers, and whether any feature is too thin to
   survive your material.
3. **You get the report.** Plain English, problem by problem, worst-first — before you spend a sheet.

---

## What we check (section — lifts straight from the validator)

- **Open paths** — the #1 cause of parts that won't release and lines that double-burn.
- **Duplicate & overlapping lines** — double burns, doubled cut time.
- **Scale & units** — is it actually the size you think, and will it fit your bed?
- **Thin features** — anything narrow enough to burn through your material, flagged against the thickness you enter.
- **Cut vs engrave** — filled shapes that will engrave when you meant to cut.
- **Hidden rasters** — a bitmap smuggled into a "cut" file that your laser can't cut.
- **Node bloat** — paths dense enough to make the head stutter or the file choke on import.

## The honest part (section — this box is a feature, not fine print)

**What we won't pretend.**

We can't see your laser. Power, speed, focus, lens, air assist, and the exact board you loaded all affect the
final burn, and those are yours to dial in. LaserReady guarantees the *file* is structurally sound — closed,
clean, correctly scaled and layered. It's the difference between "the file is good" and "the cut is perfect."
We do the first one, completely. The second one is you and your machine — as it should be.

## FAQ

**Is it really free?**
The checker is, and stays that way. It's how we prove LaserReady's worth before asking you for anything.

**Do I need an account?**
Not for your first check. Drop a file and go. An email gets you on the early-access list and saved reports.

**What files can I check?**
SVG and DXF today. Bought, AI-made, or self-drawn — source doesn't matter. Raster (PNG/JPG) support and
one-click repair come with Phase 1.

**Does it work with my machine?**
The check is machine-agnostic. When export launches, you'll pick a profile (LightBurn, Glowforge, or a
generic colored-layer SVG) so the file comes out in the exact convention your laser expects.

**Do you keep my designs?**
Your file is checked and not shared. When export launches, we keep a copy of *what we generated for you*
only — that's what backs the guarantee — and nothing else.
